{% trans "How are schools allocated?" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Each school points to its top ranked participant in its priority list and each participant points to his/her top ranked school in his/her preference list. Whenever a pointer cycle is found, all participant in the cycle are assigned to the schools they point to. The capacities of all schools in the cycle are reduced by one. All participant that were assigned in this step and all schools with depleted capacities are removed. The procedure continues with the remaining participant and schools. The procedure terminates when no more cycles are found. In other words, the mechanism at hands trades preferences and priorities between schools and participants. {% endblocktrans %}
